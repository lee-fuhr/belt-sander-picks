Quick answer

The Makita 9924DB is the best belt sander for most people, offering a perfect blend of power, control, and durability for a wide range of sanding tasks.

The Bosch BS100 is a strong runner-up for those who prioritize dust management, while the Wen 6503 is the top pick for tight budgets and the Festool RTS 400 is ideal for professionals demanding the finest finish.

A good belt sander transforms rough lumber into smooth, flat surfaces in minutes. Whether you’re flattening a workbench, removing old paint, or shaping a piece of furniture, the right machine saves time and reduces fatigue. Choosing a belt sander comes down to a few key factors: motor power (which determines how aggressively it removes material), dust collection (because fine sawdust is no joke), belt size (3-by-21 inches is the standard, but others exist for specialty work), and ergonomics, a well-balanced sander with a comfortable grip lets you work longer without cramping. For this roundup, we looked at owner feedback, professional reviews, and long-term reliability reports to pick five models that cover every need. We considered track record, dust collection effectiveness, ease of belt changes, and real-world build quality. Best for specific needs

Best for heavy stock removal

When you need to flatten a slab, remove deep paint layers, or shape wood fast, aggressive removal power matters most. The Makita 9924DB’s strong motor and wide belt make short work of rough material while still offering enough control to avoid gouging. Its simple design and reliable tracking keep you focused on the work. Our pick: Makita 9924DB (Best Overall).

Best for fine finishing

For the last passes before sandpaper grits go above 120, you want a sander that’s smooth, well-balanced, and produces minimal swirl marks. The Festool RTS 400 excels here with its precise speed control and near-zero dust emission. It’s the go-to tool for achieving a flawless surface on showpiece furniture. Our pick: Festool RTS 400 (Best Premium).

Best for portability and cord-free use

Working on scaffolding, second-story decks, or just far from an outlet? The Milwaukee M18 Fuel belt sander gives you corded-like power without the cord. Its compact size makes it easy to carry and maneuver, and swapping batteries keeps you going all day as long as you have spares. It’s the ultimate job-site companion. Our pick: Milwaukee M18 Fuel Belt Sander (Best Compact).

Our verdict

No single belt sander does everything perfectly, but the Makita 9924DB comes closest for most woodworkers, it’s powerful, reliable, and a pleasure to use. If dust extraction is your prime concern, the Bosch BS100 is a fantastic runner-up. On a budget, the Wen 6503 delivers solid performance for the price, while the Festool RTS 400 is the finishing specialist for pros who demand the cleanest results. And if you need freedom from cords, the Milwaukee M18 Fuel keeps up with the corded pack. Match your choice to your most common sanding task, and you’ll have a tool that earns its place on your bench.
